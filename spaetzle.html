Spätzle

Soft German egg noodles finished in butter and herbs.

Story

As a chef when you are working through the ranks you come across mentors that shape your food journey and open up your culinary horizons. Peter Stiegler for me was one of those people and I was in charge of preparing the spaetzle every night at his restaurant Steiglers. Peter, who was the best leader I ever met, always reminded me every night to never be shy with the seasoning.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups (260g) all-purpose flour
  • 4 large eggs
  • ½ cup (120ml) whole milk (plus 1–2 Tbsp if needed)
  • 1 tsp kosher salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 2–3 Tbsp butter (for finishing)
  • Chives or parsley, finely chopped (optional)

Method

  1. Bring a large pot of well-salted water to a rolling boil.
  2. Whisk eggs and milk; add flour, salt, and a few grinds of pepper. Beat vigorously until a thick, elastic batter forms (it should fall slowly from a spoon). If too stiff, add a splash of milk.
  3. Working in batches, press batter through a spätzle maker or the large holes of a sturdy colander directly into the boiling water.
  4. Cook 1–2 minutes until the noodles float and look set. Scoop into an ice bath or a lightly oiled tray to stop cooking. Repeat with remaining batter.
  5. To serve, melt butter in a wide pan until foamy. Add spätzle and sauté 2–3 minutes until lightly golden at the edges. Season assertively (don’t be shy!) and finish with herbs.

Chef Notes

  • Season your boiling water like pasta water—it matters.
  • Beat the batter hard: gluten development gives that bouncy chew.
  • Batch the boil, then finish all portions à la minute in butter for the best texture.
